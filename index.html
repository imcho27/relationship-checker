England and Wales: decree absolute (before April 2022) or final order (April 2022 onwards). Scotland: decree of divorce. Northern Ireland: Decree Absolute or Final Order. Or a death certificate if your previous partner passed away.
UK law does not permit marriage between close relatives. This includes parent and child, siblings (including half-siblings), grandparent and grandchild, aunt or uncle and nephew or niece, and adoptive parent and child. Step-siblings are not included.

Immigration History

Your immigration history is checked by the Home Office. Answer accurately — most issues can be addressed with the right evidence and explanation.

These questions are standard on all UK visa applications. A previous issue does not automatically prevent an application — what matters is how it is addressed.

Overstaying is a risk factor but is not automatically fatal. There are legal exceptions and a documented reason can significantly help your case.
Important — High Risk

A previous removal or deportation order is a serious matter. However this does not automatically prevent an application — exceptions exist under Article 8 family life rights. Your assessment will continue but will show this as a high risk factor. We strongly recommend speaking to a solicitor before applying.

Criminal convictions can affect an application on suitability grounds. We strongly recommend getting professional legal advice before applying.
